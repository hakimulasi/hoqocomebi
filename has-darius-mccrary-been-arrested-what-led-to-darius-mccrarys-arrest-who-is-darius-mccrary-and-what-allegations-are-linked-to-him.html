Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, September 11, 2024

News: Darius McCrary, renowned for portraying Eddie Winslow on the beloved 90s show “Family Matters,” has encountered fresh legal woes. On November 27, the 47-year-old actor was apprehended on a felony charge related to unpaid child support. This latest incident follows an arrest on October 25 in Redondo Beach, California, due to an out-of-state warrant. Though the link between these events remains uncertain, it’s apparent that McCrary has been contending with legal complications for an extended period.

A Staggering Amount of Child Support Arrearage

McCrary’s most recent arrest stemmed from an alarming $52,788 in unpaid child support arrears by March 12, 2019. This substantial amount led to his detention. This isn’t his initial encounter with such allegations; back in 2015, he faced similar charges. Despite his plea of not guilty, McCrary presently finds himself incarcerated, with a bail amount of $13,197.01 set.

From Sibling Reunion to Legal Troubles

Despite his legal troubles, McCrary expressed his joy at reuniting with his “Family Matters” co-star Kellie Shanygne Williams in October 2020 for the film “Christmas in Carolina,” where they portrayed siblings. However, this recent arrest adds to a series of legal issues that the talented actor has faced in recent years.

A Rising Star on “Family Matters”

Darius McCrary’s depiction of Eddie Winslow in “Family Matters” garnered him immense acclaim and a cherished spot in fans’ affections. Running from 1989 to 1998, the sitcom revolved around the Winslow family and their encounters with the eccentric neighbor, Steve Urkel. McCrary’s compelling portrayal of Eddie Winslow significantly added to the show’s triumph, cementing his reputation as a gifted actor.
