Career

In 1986, Theresa May made an entry into politics as a councilor for the UK Borough of Merton. Between 1988 to 1990, she worked as the Chairman of Education. In July 2002, she became the first women chairman of the ‘Conservative Party.’ May became the Shadow Secretary of State for “Transport and the Environment” and for “The Family” in 2003, and 2004 respectively. In 2005, saw was appointed as Shadow Secretary of State for “The Culture, Media and Sport.”

She was progressing in her career at a fast pace. In 2010, UK Prime Minister, ‘David Cameron,’ appointed as “Home Secretary and Minister” for Women and Equality.
