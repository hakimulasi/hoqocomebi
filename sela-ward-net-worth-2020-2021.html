Early Life & Biography

Annie Kate, a housewife, and Granberry Holland Ward, Jr., an electrical engineer, had Sela Ward on 11th July 1956 in Meridian, Mississippi. She had one younger sister, Jenna, and two more youthful brothers, Brock and Granberry Holland Ward III.

Sela completed her graduation from the University of Alabama. She was famous for her university too. She got the title of Homecoming Queen, she was a part of Crimson Tide cheerleader and also joined Chi-Omega sorority.

Personal Life

Ward tied the knot with entrepreneur Howard Elliot Sherman on May 23, 1992. She had two beautiful children, Austin and Anabella, with him.

During a holiday trip home to Mississippi in 1997, she met two foster children, which made her think about the abused and neglected children who needed care love and safety. So, she decided to work for these children by initiating and partially funding the creation of Hope Village for Children, permanent group home and emergency shelter, in her hometown Meridian in Jan 2002. In 2002, Ward published her autobiography, ‘Homesick: A Memoir.’

Career

Before starting her career as an actress, Ward did some modeling to supplement her income. She did T.V. commercials promoting Maybelline cosmetics and recruited by the Wilhelmina Agency.

Ward moved to California in the dream of becoming an actress. In 1991, she landed on a role as Teddy Reed in a series called ‘ Sisters,’ which helped her in winning her first Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series in 1994. She acted in one of the top films of 1993, ‘The Fugitive.’ She played the role of late T.V. journalist Jessica Savitch in 1995 T.V. movie ‘Almost Golden: The Jessica Savitch Story,’ a film that remains Lifetime’s most-watched T.V. movie till date.
