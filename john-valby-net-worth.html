John Valby Full Overview and Wiki

Early Career and Transition to Solo Act

Valby was born and raised in Western New York and attended school in Rochester, New York. He learned to play the piano at the age of seven, taught by two nuns. His interest in becoming a performer was evident from the age of ten.

After graduating from Middlebury College in Middlebury, Vermont, in 1966, he initially considered a career as a philosophy teacher but soon realized his true passion lay in musical entertainment.

Valby joined various rock bands in his early career, but these bands often broke up, leading him to become a solo piano act. His music initially consisted mostly of rock and roll and honky-tonk tunes.

However, a significant turning point in his career came in May 1974 when he added some dirty songs of his own into his set. The positive response to these songs led Valby to focus more on adult material.

Rise to Fame as Dr. Dirty

Valby claimed that he was named Dr. Dirty by the National Entertainment Conference, a booking organization for the college circuit. He initially disliked the name because he felt it takes away from the element of surprise. His obscene act was partly a reaction to the Catholic repression he faced during his childhood.

Musical Productions and Film Appearance

In addition to his blue comedy act, Valby has recorded and produced pop and rock albums for himself and other artists. Notably, he worked with Buffalo Sabres defenseman Jim Schoenfeld, with whom Valby recorded two albums, Schony (1972) and The Key is Love (1974).

He also had a small role as a piano player in a Tijuana brothel in the 1983 film Losin’ It, which featured an early performance by Tom Cruise.

Discography

John Valby’s discography is extensive, with albums ranging from 1974 to 2018. Some of his notable albums include Philosophical Bull Shit (1974), Dirt (1975), Hotel Buffalo (1977), Concerto for Piano, Voice and 500 Screaming Assholes (1978), and many more, showcasing his prolific nature as a musician and comedian.
