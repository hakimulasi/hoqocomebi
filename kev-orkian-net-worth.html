Kev Orkian Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Education

Kev Orkian, born Kevork Kapikyan, is a shining example of a multi-talented artist whose roots lie in North London. Of Armenian descent, Orkian’s journey began in the vibrant streets of London, where he embraced his British-Armenian heritage.

His academic path led him to Brunel University, laying the foundation for a career that would later see him blend comedy with musical genius.

Rise to Fame

Orkian’s early career saw him touring the UK, supporting some of the biggest names in British comedy. His unique style, a blend of humor and musical talent, quickly set him apart.

His performances in West-End musicals like Fame and Happy Days showcased his versatility, while his stand-up comedy acts in clubs like Jongleurs highlighted his innate ability to connect with audiences.

Britain’s Got Talent and Beyond

2010 was a pivotal year for Kev as he took the Britain’s Got Talent stage by storm. His piano comedy act, especially his rendition of Elton John’s ‘I’m Still Standing’, won him acclaim and a spot in the semi-finals.
