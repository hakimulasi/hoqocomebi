Is Alex Galchenyuk Arrested?

Yes, Alex Galchenyuk was arrested. In July, he got into trouble with the police in Arizona after being involved in a hit-and-run incident. The bodycam footage released by the Scottsdale Police showed Galchenyuk making threats to the officers and using racial slurs. He was accused of driving erratically, crashing into a parking lot sign and another vehicle. After his arrest, the Arizona Coyotes terminated his contract.

Galchenyuk pleaded guilty to one misdemeanor threatening charge, and the other charges were dismissed. He apologized for his behavior and entered an alcohol recovery program as part of his sentence. Despite this incident, he later signed with SKA Saint Petersburg in the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL).

Who is Alex Galchenyuk?

Alex Galchenyuk is a professional ice hockey player born on February 12, 1994. Known for his versatility, Galchenyuk plays as a center and has had a notable career in the National Hockey League (NHL). He was drafted third overall by the Montreal Canadiens in the 2012 NHL Entry Draft. Despite being of Belarusian descent, Galchenyuk was born in the United States, and he has represented the United States internationally in hockey competitions.

Throughout his career, Galchenyuk has played for various NHL teams, including the Montreal Canadiens, Arizona Coyotes, Pittsburgh Penguins, Minnesota Wild, Ottawa Senators, Toronto Maple Leafs, and Colorado Avalanche. His journey in professional hockey has showcased his skill and adaptability on the ice. Outside of the NHL, Galchenyuk joined SKA Saint Petersburg in the Kontinental Hockey League (KHL).
